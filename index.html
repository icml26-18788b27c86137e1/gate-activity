TrasMuon Gate Diagnostics: How Column-Level Damping Responds to Energy Bursts

TrasMuon augms Muon-family optimizers with a column-level energy gate that detects and damps anomalous gradient concentrations before Newton-Schulz orthogonalization mixes the update. At each gating step, the per-column energy ratio $r_j = E_j / (E_{\mathrm{ref}} + \epsilon)$ measures how much each column's energy deviates from a smoothed reference. Columns exceeding a threshold are damped by a log-hinge gate $c_j = \mathrm{clip}\bigl(1/(1 + \alpha \log(1+r_j)), c_{\min}, 1\bigr)$, which is strictly damping-only $(c_j \leq 1)$. The applied gate is temporally smoothed via EMA and schedule-free blending: $c_{\mathrm{used}} = (1-\rho),c_{\mathrm{ema}} + \rho,c_{\mathrm{avg}}$.

The following diagnostic quantities are tracked per layer per step:

  • active_rate: fraction of columns whose energy ratio exceeds the trigger threshold ($r_j > k$). Rising active_rate signals that the gate has detected an energy burst.
  • damp_rate: fraction of columns with any measurable damping ($c_j < 1$). A superset of active_rate; captures columns damped even mildly.
  • floor_rate: fraction of columns at maximum damping ($cj = c{\min}$). High floor_rate indicates a strong burst saturating the gate.
  • c_used_mean: mean applied gate coefficient across all columns. Values near 1.0 indicate the gate is nearly dormant (stationary regime); lower values indicate active damping.
  • r_ratio: ratio of max to 95th-percentile energy ratio ($r{\max} / r{q95}$). Measures how localized the energy concentration is; high values indicate a small number of outlier columns.

How to read the plots. In stationary regimes, all rates remain near zero and c_used_mean stays close to 1 — the gate imposes no overhead. When a burst event occurs (e.g., ROI shift in PINNs, or gradient outlier in LM training), r_ratio spikes first, followed by rising active_rate and damp_rate, and a corresponding drop in c_used_mean. This closed-loop response — detect → damp → recover — is the core mechanism by which TrasMuon suppresses loss spikes without interfering with normal optimization.
